Verbal Reasoning

This section tests your comfort level with text and grammar. The Verbal Reasoning section of the GMAT Test Pattern 2016 contains three types of questions:

a. Reading Comprehension.
b. Critical Reasoning.
c. Sentence Equivalence.

Analytical Writing


The Analytical Writing section measures your ability to articulate complex ideas clearly and effectively.
Analytical Writing Assessment: according to the GMAT test pattern 2016, the Analytical Writing Assessment measures your ability to think critically and to communicate your ideas. During the assessment test, you are asked to analyse the reasoning behind a given argument and write a critique of that argument. It covers jsut one topic and candidates are given 30 minutes to complete the test.
